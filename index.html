Hello! My name is

Edward Jackson

I'm a recent graduate with a Master's degree in Robotics Engineering from Worcester Polytechnic Institute. Throughout my academic journey, I have gained hands-on experience working with a diverse range of robotic systems, including mobile manipulators, quadrupeds, and soft robots. My studies have equipped me with a strong foundation in robotics and the skills necessary to tackle complex challenges in this field.

I am most interested in high Degree of Freedom (DOF) robotic systems with novel control implementations to achieve their tasks. In my undergraduate Major Qualifying Project team, we created a 13 DOF modular mobile manipulator- the first of its kind that we're aware of. In my Graduate year, I continued this project by implementing a nonlinear Quadratic Program Holistic Control approach to maximize the Yoshikawa manipulability metric while avoiding joint limits and velocities.

I am currently in search of full-time employment in a diverse, engaging, and challenging environment that fosters growth academically and intellectually. Thank you for taking the time to explore my e-Portfolio.

Please find some of my most recent projects and contact information below.
